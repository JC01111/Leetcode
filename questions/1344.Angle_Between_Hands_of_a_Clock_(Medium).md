## 1344. Angle Between Hands of a Clock (Medium)

Link: https://leetcode.com/problems/angle-between-hands-of-a-clock/

<br>

### Question:
Given two numbers, `hour` and `minutes`. Return the smaller angle (in degrees) formed between the `hour` and the `minute` hand.

**Example 1:**
> **Input:** hour = 3, minutes = 0 <br>
> **Output:** 90.0 <br>
> **Explanation:** The hour hand is at 3 and the minute hand is at 12

**Example 2:**
> **Input:** hour = 3, minutes = 15 <br>
> **Output:** 7.5 <br>
> **Explanation:** The hour hand is at 3 and 1/4, and the minute hand is at 3

**Example 3:**
> **Input:** hour = 4, minutes = 50 <br>
> **Output:** 155.0 <br>
> **Explanation:** The hour hand is at 4 and 5/6, and the minute hand is at 10

<br>

#### Constraints:
* 1 <= hour <= 12
* 0 <= minutes <= 59

<br>

### Walk-through:
The key is to calculate the degree position of both hands:
1. **Hour hand**: Moves 30° per hour (360° / 12 hours) and 0.5° per minute (30° / 60 minutes). So: `(hour % 12) * 30 + (minutes / 2)`
2. **Minute hand**: Moves 6° per minute (360° / 60 minutes). So: `minutes * 6`
3. Find the absolute difference between the two angles
4. Since we want the smaller angle, take the minimum of the difference and `360 - difference` to handle cases where the clockwise angle is larger than 180°

<br>

### Python Solution:
```python
class Solution:
    def angleClock(self, hour: int, minutes: int) -> float:
        # Hour arm: 30 degrees for 1 hour
        # Min arm: move 360 degrees in 1 hr
        # Degrees for hour = hour % 12 * (min / 2)
        # Degrees for min = 6 degrees
        # 1. Use the larger degree to minus smaller degree
        # 2. If we calculate the degree from another way is smaller, we should use that (360 - current degree)

        # hour = hour % 12 * 30 + (min / 2)
        # min = min * 6
        # max(hour, min) - min(hour, min)
        # TC: O(1), SC: O(1)

        hourDegree = (hour % 12) * 30 + (minutes / 2)
        minDegree = minutes * 6
        res = max(hourDegree, minDegree) - min(hourDegree, minDegree)
        res = min(res, 360 - res)
        return res
```
**Time Complexity:** $O(1)$ <br>
**Space Complexity:** $O(1)$
